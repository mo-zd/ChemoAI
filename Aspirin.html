Drug Properties for Aspirin

  • Target disease: Pain, fever, inflammation, and blood clotting disorders
  • Molecular weight: 180.16 g/mol
  • Pharmacokinetic profile: Rapidly absorbed from the stomach and small intestine, metabolized in the liver, and excreted in the urine
  • Toxicity: Overdose can cause gastrointestinal bleeding, kidney damage, and other serious complications
  • Mechanism of action: Inhibits the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in pain, inflammation, and blood clotting
  • Side effects: Common side effects include stomach upset, heartburn, and nausea
  • Potential drug interactions: Aspirin can interact with other drugs, including blood thinners, corticosteroids, and certain antibiotics
